Tickit: How it Came About

Scrolling through my photos, I was trying to find the day I saw Damian Lillard drop 61 points on the Warriors live. It took minutes of scrolling back and forth until I finally found it, and then I thought, what would be a better, faster way for me to save and share my memories with friends? I had collected a ticket stub from the game but lost it in my clutter of stuff, and regret not having anything to remember it by.

Role: UX Researcher, UI Designer

Timeline: 3 weeks

Tools: Figma, Adobe Illustrator

User Flow

The main challenge we were facing was how to come up with a user interface that was best suited for an accessible and quick user experience. We wanted to introduce new ways to navigate memories, while maintaining a sort of familiarity to the way people found their way around. This is why we introduced a navigation bar with 5 main features, each serving the purpose of saving, sharing, and revisiting memories.
